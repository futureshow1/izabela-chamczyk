Izabela Chamczyk

Bio

Izabela Chamczyk (born 1980).

Graduated with distinction in painting from the Academy of Fine Arts in Wroclaw in 2009, also studied at the Academy of Fine Arts in Poznan. Painter – performance artist – video artist. She creates processual, sensual, emotional art that references sexuality and corporeality. She stands out for her originality and courage in exploring the boundaries of art, making her work unique and significant in the context of contemporary artistic discourse.

She coined the term performative painting, combining image with time-based performance. Through her art, she strives to reach the sphere of the outside world, beyond the gallery.

Izabela Chamczyk is a three-time scholarship recipient of the Minister of Culture and National Heritage, a finalist in prestigious competitions such as the Vordemberge-Gildewart Foundation competition at MOCAK, Artistic Journey of Hestia, Bielska Jesień Painting Biennale, E. Geppert competition in Wroclaw, and Triennale of Young Artists in Oronsko.

Her works can be found in collections including: National Museum in Wroclaw, National Museum in Lublin, MCSW Elektrownia in Radom, Imago Mundi Collection (Italy), PKO Bank Polski, Ergo Hestia, CSW Torun, Archive of Performance at the Museum of Modern Art in Warsaw, and many private collections in Poland and abroad.
